## Scarce-Layer Lens

After selecting an industry, rank layers by scarcity:

1. Demand urgency: which customer budget is forced, not optional?
2. Bottleneck: which layer prevents capacity, speed, yield, compliance, or cost from improving?
3. Supplier count: how many credible suppliers exist?
4. Qualification: how long does customer validation or regulatory approval take?
5. Expansion difficulty: capex, permits, process know-how, skilled labor, raw material purity, or grid connection.
6. Pricing power: can the layer defend margin when demand rises?
7. Evidence: filings, orders, backlog, capex, tenders, utilization, inventory, cash conversion.

## Ranking Heuristic

Use this qualitative scoring when a numeric score is useful:

- Scarcity and control: 0-30
- Demand pressure from current signals: 0-20
- Evidence quality: 0-20
- Timing and near-term verification path: 0-15
- Valuation/positioning room: 0-10
- Governance/liquidity penalty: 0 to -15

Do not let a high macro score override weak company evidence.
